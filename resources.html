Insurance and Medicare/Medicaid Information

We accept multiple insurance plans to make our home health services accessible. Below is a summary of coverage options and how home health care is covered:

Medicare Coverage

  • 100% coverage for approved home health services
  • Doctor must certify intermittent skilled care need
  • Must be homebound (difficult to leave without assistance)
  • Face-to-face evaluation required
  • Services by Medicare-certified agency only

For more details on Medicare eligibility and benefits, visit the official Medicare website.

Florida Medicaid

  • Covers home health services for eligible individuals
  • Includes skilled nursing visits and therapy
  • Based on medical necessity
  • Income/assets criteria apply

To learn more or apply for Florida Medicaid, visit the Florida Department of Children and Families' ACCESS Florida portal.

Private Insurance

  • Most major insurance plans accepted
  • HMOs, PPOs, and managed care plans
  • Coverage verification service available
  • Staff assistance with benefits and authorizations

If you have questions about your coverage or eligibility, feel free to call us. We can help you understand your plan's home health benefits and even direct you to the right resources (such as Medicare or Medicaid information tools) for further information.

Downloadable Forms

For your convenience, we provide important forms that you can download, print, or fill out to expedite your care process. Below are the forms available (in PDF format and, where noted, as fillable electronic forms):

Referral Form

For physicians, hospitals, or clinics to refer a patient. Includes sections for patient information, diagnosis, requested services, and physician signature. Available as printable PDF and electronic form.

New Patient Intake Packet

Comprehensive admission packet containing patient information forms, consent for treatment, medical history questionnaire, and patient rights & responsibilities.

HIPAA Consent/Release Form

Authorize our agency to share your protected health information with designated family members or caregivers in compliance with HIPAA regulations.

Medication List Template

Simple worksheet to record current medications, dosages, times of day, and prescribing doctor. Useful for doctor visits and emergencies.

Caregiver Daily Checklist

Track daily care tasks like meals, medications, exercises, vital signs, and includes a notes section for improved communication among family members and care team.

Download Instructions: Click on the name of any form to download it. You may fill it out and email/fax it to us, or have it ready for the staff during the initial assessment. If you need a form in a different format or have trouble downloading, please contact our office and we can send it to you directly.

External Resources

We have gathered a list of trusted external resources, including official health agencies and organizations, to provide you with additional information and support.

Alliance for Aging

Miami-Dade based private, not-for-profit agency connecting seniors to services for meals, recreation, adult day care, personal care, legal help, and transportation.

Florida Department of Elder Affairs

State agency dedicated to services for Florida's seniors, offering programs like National Family Caregiver Support, aging in place resources, and Elder Helpline.

U.S. Department of Health & Human Services

Federal department overseeing public health, healthcare services, and programs like Medicare/Medicaid with information on national health policies.

Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services

Federal agency administering Medicare, Medicaid and CHIP, providing detailed coverage guidelines and Medicare Care Compare tools.

Florida Department of Health

State health department with resources on public health programs, statewide health advisories, and services specific to Florida residents.

American Heart Association

Leading nonprofit organization providing educational articles, healthy living tips, and support resources for heart disease and stroke recovery.

Alzheimer's Association

National organization offering 24/7 helpline, caregiver tips, local support group finder, and latest information on Alzheimer's care strategies.

American Medical Association

Professional association offering public health education, articles on health conditions, and tools like doctor finders and health glossaries.

Family Caregiver Alliance

Nonprofit organization providing education, caregiving tips, webinars, support group information, and fact sheets for family caregivers.

National Institute on Aging

Government research-based advice on home modifications, fall prevention, and independent living for seniors aging in place.
